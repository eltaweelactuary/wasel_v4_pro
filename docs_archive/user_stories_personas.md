# Documentation: User Personas & User Stories

**Project:** Wasel v4 Pro  
**Stage:** Phase 1 (Impact Analysis)

---

## 👥 User Personas

### 1. Zain (The Primary User)
*   **Role:** University student with hearing impairment.
*   **Need:** Wants to follow lectures and interact with peers in real-time.
*   **Pain Point:** Traditional interpreters are expensive and not always available.
*   **Benefit from Wasel:** Can use his smartphone to translate signs to text during group discussions.

### 2. Sarah (The Service Provider)
*   **Role:** Bank teller.
*   **Need:** Needs to provide equal service to hearing-impaired customers.
*   **Pain Point:** Communication barriers lead to frustration and errors.
*   **Benefit from Wasel:** Uses the "Text → Video" feature to show sign language instructions to the customer via a tablet.

### 3. Omar (The Developer/Student)
*   **Role:** CS student interested in SLT.
*   **Need:** Wants to contribute or expand the vocabulary for regional dialects.
*   **Benefit from Wasel:** Uses the "Modular Architecture" to add new skeletal DNA without rewriting the entire engine.

---

## 📝 Key User Stories (Phase 1)

| As a... | I want to... | So that... |
|---|---|---|
| **Zain** | Type English/Urdu text and see an avatar sign it. | I can learn the correct signs for academic terms. |
| **Sarah** | Upload a video of a signer. | I can understand the customer's request instantly. |
| **Zain** | See a live camera feed with recognized text overlays. | I can communicate naturally without looking away from the speaker. |
| **Omar** | Sync models via Cloud Storage. | I can access the same trained data across different devices. |

---
> [!TIP]
> **Product Perspective:** Wasel v4 Pro isn't just a translator; it's an **Accessibility Bridge** that empowers both signers and their listeners.
